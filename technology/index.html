Technology

Communicating, navigating and monitoring underwater is a challenging problem and entails a number of hurdles specific to the domain. Adopting land based technologies like Wi-Fiâ„¢, GPS is not possible as they use electromagnetic waves that are rapidly absorbed in water. Another possible solution is to use acoustics, which comes with a variety of challenges such as low-bit rate, low bandwidth, loss from echoes etc. Communications, the key enabler for networking, navigation and sensing, require a high level of flexibility over physical layer signaling, sensing as well as granular control over timing (transmission & reception).

Software-defined Modem

The SubNero Underwater Modem provides a flexible platform for a variety of underwater networks and applications. With substantial computing power packed into a compact form factor, users are able to implement and deploy complex algorithms in the modem, hence allowing robust communication between underwater nodes as well as driving the innovation of new protocols and applications. The modem provides options for customization and extension at many levels, allowing network protocols as well as physical layer algorithms to be implemented and tested easily.
